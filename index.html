Stan Smith is the title character of American Dad!
Stan has worked for the Central Intelligence Agency since the 80s, as a field agent and weapons expert under Deputy-Director Avery Bullock. In this role, his work has included the interrogation of possible terrorists. Stan is always on alert for terrorist activity, at work, and in his home life.

Francine Smith

Francine Smith (née Ling; formerly Dawson) is the wife of Stan Smith and the mother of Hayley Smith and Steve Smith.

Hayley Smith

Hayley Dreamsmasher Smith-Fischer is the eldest child of Francine and Stan Smith. Hayley, age 19, has a younger brother, Steve. They reside in Langley Falls, Virginia. The family also has a fish named Klaus and a boarder, a space alien named Roger. Hayley’s maternal grandparents are Bah Bah Ling and Ma Ma Ling. Jack Smith is Hayley’s paternal grandfather. Roger is one of the main characters in the animated comedy series American Dad!. He is a space alien whose appearance resembles that of the greys living with the Smith family. Roger is sarcastic, surly, amoral, self-centered, hedonistic, alcoholic, and dresses up in a variety of personas.

For God’s sake

Source: For God's sake. I just wanna pick up a pack of smokes.

Definition: An exclamation of anger, surprise, or impatience. when and where all other reasons and arguments fail, we tend to turn to God …. and plead “for God’s sake” ….. as if that’s the ultimate excuse that we may put forward to convince a person or justify an action or whatever…..; It’s an English expression. It’s like saying “Oh, please!” It is used as an expression to strongly emphasize what you’re asking for. For example, “For God’s sake! Shut up.” or “For God’s sake! Stop.” Though it’s not nice using the name of God in vain expressions. So just use “Oh, please! instead.

Examples:

  • For God's sake, don’t scare me like that!
  • Put some pants on, for God's sake!

pick up a pack of smokes

Source: For God’s sake. I just wanna pick up a pack of smokes.

Type: phrasal verb

Definition: to buy or get (something)

Examples:

  • Have you picked up a copy of her new CD yet?
  • On Tuesdays, he picks up dinner at our favorite restaurant and brings it home.
  • She stopped to pick up a few groceries at the supermarket.

Daily English 202110

chicken out

Source: I just wish it was a little cheaper. Every time I think about buying a license I realize it’s like 30-40 USD more than I expect and I chicken out.

Definition: to decide not to do something because you are afraid

Definition(cn): (由于害怕)退出，临阵退缩

Type: phrasal verb

Examples:

  • He was going to ask her on a date, but he chickened out at the last minute. | (他本打算约她出来，但临阵退缩了。)

chicken (adj.)

Definition: not used before a noun [more chicken; most chicken] informal; too afraid to do something

Definition(cn): 胆怯；懦弱；怯弱

Examples:

  • He’s too chicken [=cowardly] to stand up to them. | (他过于懦弱，不敢与他们抗争。)
  • You’re chicken, aren’t you? | (你是个胆小鬼，对不对？)

chicken (n.)

Definition: [count] informal : a person who is afraid : coward

Definition(cn): 懦夫；胆小鬼

Examples:

  • It’s just a spider, you chicken! | (就是一只蜘蛛而已，你这个胆小鬼！)
  • Don’t be such a big chicken. | (别这么胆小。)

move up the deadline

Source: he’s moved up the deadline for states to make Covid-19 vaccines available to all Americans

Source Url: link

Context: President Joe Biden is set to announce Tuesday he’s moved up the deadline for states to make Covid-19 vaccines available to all Americans, directing governors to drop eligibility requirements by April 19 rather than May 1, CNN first reported and Forbes confirmed.

Definition: To change the date or time of some scheduled event to an earlier date or time.

Type: phrasal verb

Examples:

  • Several of our friends were leaving town in July, so we moved up our party to early June.
  • To avoid staying too late tonight, we should move the meeting up a few hours.

bring forward the meeting

Source: The situation is quite urgent, so let’s bring forward the meeting to this afternoon”

Definition: (British) If you bring forward a meeting or event, you arrange for it to take place at an earlier date or time than had been planned.

Definition(cn): 提前

Examples::

  • He had to bring forward an 11 o’clock meeting so that he could get to the funeral on time. [VERB PARTICLE noun]
  • The election date had to be brought forward by two months

push the meeting back

Source: I’d like to push the meeting back a few hours.

Definition: to change (a planned event) to start at a later date or time

Definition(cn): 推迟

Type: phrasal verb

Examples:

  • The date of the meeting has been pushed back from Tuesday to Thursday. | (这次会议的日期从星期二推迟到了星期四。)
  • The start time of the game has been pushed back from 1 p.m. to 3 p.m. | (这场比赛的开赛时间从下午1点推迟到了3点.)

call off the event

Source: We are going to call off the event because we don’t have enough participants

Definition: cancel an arrangement;

Definition(cn): 取消

Type: phrasal verb

Examples:

  • The business trip has been called off.

put off this discussion

Source: Let’s put off this discussion until tomorrow.

Definition: to decide that (something) will happen at a later time : postpone; For more formal arrangements, ‘postpone’ is probably the better option here, but for slightly less formal situations such as discussions, stand-up meetings or quick calls, put off is a great alternative. ‘Put off’ is great also for postponing individual tasks. For individual tasks, the word ‘postpone’ sounds too strict and timetabled, so ‘put off’ is the ideal option here.

Definition(cn): 推迟

Type: phrasal verb

Examples:

  • Let’s put off this discussion until tomorrow.
  • I sometimes put off difficult tasks until the last minute.

基础功能

Features:

  • key words or expressions: 单词或表达
  • 类别:单词, phrase/expression, phrasal verb, Idiom

  • 该单词或表达在上面context中的英文释义
  • 该单词或表达在上面context中的中文释义

  • 标签

  • 搜索: 单词, 表达, 句式结构(关键词)
  • 创建时间, 更新时间
  • review模式: 不同的review profiles: 可以设定显示哪些字段/内容
  • 练习巩固:
    • 填空: 例句或造句中把单词表达抹去,放入一个练习池中, 匹配正确的答案. (在语境中巩固单词表达)
